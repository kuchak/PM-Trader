What Is Polymarket?

Polymarket is a prediction market platform where you buy and sell shares in the outcomes of real-world events. Shares are priced between $0.00 and $1.00 — the price represents the market's implied probability. If you buy "Yes" at $0.90 and the event happens, you get $1.00 back (profit: $0.10). If it doesn't happen, you get $0.00 (loss: $0.90).

Our bots trade on live sports (NBA, NCAA, ATP/WTA tennis), crypto price movements (BTC/ETH/XRP 15-minute and 1-hour windows), and collect data on weather markets. All trades go through Polymarket's Central Limit Order Book (CLOB).

Key Lessons & What Didn't Work

1. High win rate ≠ profit 85% WR across 1,214 trades and still net negative. When you buy at 90c+ and sell at 99-100c, your upside is capped at ~10c per share. But your downside is the full 90c+ when a market resolves against you. You need >90% WR just to break even at these entry prices.
2. 1-hour crypto markets are a trap They look safe (88%+ WR) but produce the catastrophic losses. A 90% probability can go to 0% in a single resolution tick with no time to exit. 15m markets are actually safer because the stop-loss at 82% has time to trigger.
3. Re-entering losing markets is devastating The March 11 XRP loss ($110) came from re-entering the same market after the first exit. If the market already signaled danger, going back in doubles the damage.
4. Backtests overestimate live performance Tennis tiers were backtested at 90-96% WR on 105k matches. Live performance: 82.7%. The gap is due to: (a) slippage in entry prices, (b) auto-resolution before exits trigger, (c) top-player comebacks that are rare in aggregate but common enough to erode edge.
5. Top players come back The tennis bot's biggest blind spot. Swiatek, Medvedev, Gauff losing Set 1 is a buying opportunity for the market — not a signal that they'll lose. The strategy needs a "top player discount" that reduces position size or skips entries when the losing player is top-10.
6. Sports in-play betting works (when available) 161W/7L (95.8% WR) on live sports pre-March 10. The edge is real: once a team leads by enough in a live game, comebacks are genuinely rare. The challenge is market availability — Polymarket's sports coverage is seasonal and thin.
7. Tier S tennis is profitable 4-0, +$15.94. The highest-confidence conditions (seeded player + huge S1 margin, or very high ranking ratio) held up. The losses all came from Tier A/B/C — the lower tiers where backtested edge is thinner.
